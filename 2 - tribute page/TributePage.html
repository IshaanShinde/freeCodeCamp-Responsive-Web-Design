A Brief History

  • 1970s: The Birth of Mechanical Keyboards - Mechanical keyboards were first introduced in the 1970s with the release of the IBM Model F keyboard. These early mechanical keyboards utilized individual mechanical switches under each key, offering tactile feedback and durability.
  • 1980s: Cherry MX Switches Revolutionize the Industry - In 1983, Cherry Corporation introduced their groundbreaking Cherry MX mechanical switches, which became a standard in mechanical keyboards. These switches offered different types of tactile feedback and actuation forces, catering to individual preferences.
  • 1990s: Membrane Keyboards Gain popularity - In the 1990s, membrane keyboards gained popularity due to their lower cost and quieter operation. However, mechanical keyboards remained a favorite among enthusiasts who valued their durability and tactile feel.
  • 2010s: Resurgence of Mechanical Keyboards - Mechanical keyboards experienced a resurgence in the 2010s as the gaming and tech communities embraced them for their precision and comfort. Companies like Corsair, Razer, and Logitech began producing mechanical keyboards tailored to gamers' needs.
  • Present Day: Customization and Modern Innovations - Today, mechanical keyboards continue to evolve with a focus on customization and innovation. Keyboards with customizable switches, RGB lighting, and ergonomic designs have become common, catering to various preferences and needs of users. The popularity of mechanical keyboards shows no signs of slowing down, with enthusiasts seeking unique typing experiences and higher build quality.
